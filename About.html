I wasn't certain of what I wanted to do with my life before I started college. Although I was accepted to UCSD as a psychology student, I quickly realized that it was not what I intended to do in the long term. As I was browsing the list of majors one night, cognitive science, particularly the specialization in design and interaction, caught my attention. The more I researched about the major, the more I became interested in it. This is when I ultimately decided to change my major.

Since then, I've had the chance to join the Diabetes Design Initiative Team at the UCSD Design Lab and work as a product design intern at a start-up named Astren. I also get to participate in several design-a-thons and joined a student organization named Triton Software Engineering, where we help non-profits design and develop software essentially for social good. I had the opportunity to collaborate with specialists from several sectors, from interviewing possible stakeholders and professionals to cooperating with other designers and developers. I still have no regrets about my decision after more than two years.

I believe good design solves complex problems in a simple yet inclusive way that considers all identities. When creating for people, I keep the human-centered approach in mind in efforts to bridge the gaps between people and spur change in the world.
